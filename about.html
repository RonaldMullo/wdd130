History

The history of whitewater rafting dates back to the early 19th century, with the first recorded attempt to navigate the Snake River in 1811. However, the popularization of rafting as a sport and recreational activity occurred in the 1950s and 1960s, with the emergence of rafting companies and its inclusion in the 1972 Olympic Games. Prior to this, explorers and fishermen were already navigating rivers in rafts of their own making, laying the groundwork for the later development of modern rafting.
